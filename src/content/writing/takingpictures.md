<i>for Kitty</i><br><br> 
We have come here expectant<BR> to the quiet shore<BR> where the high spring tide<BR> strokes the bright rocks gently.<BR> We have come here expectant<br><br>to capture just one moment<BR> of fleeting time.<BR> You see with the camera's eye, where<BR> we are blind to local imperfections:<BR> smokestacks and pylons, and all<BR> the myriad small scars of man's habitation.<BR> Now you arrange us like flowers on the sea's backdrop, hoping<BR> to capture just one moment<br><br>of our togetherness.<BR> Thus will you store against<BR> the arid future the present's joy,<BR> stockpiling happiness. They lie in drawers,<BR> in bundles, those photographs. Sometimes <BR> I take them out - chasing memories<BR> of our togetherness. 
<br><br>1993</div>
