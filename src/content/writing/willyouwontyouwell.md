Darling will you still love me when<br> 
my hair is lank and stringy,<br> 
insecurity has made me clingy,<br> 
my flesh drips wrinkled from my bones<br> 
and gasps of passion become arthritic moans?<br> 
<blockquote>Will you, won't you, well?</blockquote> 
 
Well can you picture it, surfacing for air<br> 
when wheezy lungs and mouldy flesh make sex too much to bear:<br> 
will you want me then? Will you?<br> 
Will whatever else we have fulfil you?<br> 
When years have passed, everything's been said<br> 
and the friends we've shared are dead?<br> 
<blockquote>Will you, won't you, well?</blockquote><br> 
 
Well, will we be enough for each other,<br> 
friend and enemy, father and mother,<br> 
life entire, self complete, one within another?<br> 
Will you still love me then, when<br> 
my eyes are dull and you have to shout<br> 
before I understand what you are talking about?<br> 
<br> 
And when I'm dead, flesh crumbled, all forgotten,<br> 
will you dig me up and love me rotten?<br> 
<blockquote>Will you, won't you, well?</blockquote><br>
