---<div class=bodytext>I gazed out of my window for an hour today<br>
In blank-eyed expectation of your coming;<br>
And all I saw was leaves<br>
Falling from the trees;<br>
And I cursed the deadly unforgiving minute<br>
That makes such a mockery of my longing for you.<br><br>



In the back of my mind there's a memory of you<br>
Fading and distorting with the passing minute:<br>
Some half-glimpsed glorious vision through the trees<br>
And flashes of hair like autumn leaves<br>
And a joyous gasp to signify your coming.<br>
No, I do not think I will see you today.<br><br>



And dare I hope you thought of me today ?<br>
That turning aside you recalled for a minute<br>
The park in summer, plucking leaves<br>
From my hair as we laughed beneath the trees ?<br>
And shall I tomorrow be thinking of you,<br>
And write you a letter to ask you when you're coming ?<br><br>



It seems I have waited an age for your coming:<br>
Thinking of how it would be; today<br>
I tried to fill the space; each empty minute<br>
With something else - watching the leaves <br>
Of these books turning on their own. These fossilised trees<br>
That rustle like dead skin. My fingers long for you.<br><br>



Why do I bother waiting for you ?<br>
You're changeable as the trees<br>
I see outside the window losing their leaves;<br>
And the age of this place reduces life to a minute<br>
Stone in the puzzle of existence. Today<br>
I see only an age of desolation coming;<br><br>



An age of joylessness, of emptiness coming;<br>
A skeleton world shorn of flowers, of trees,<br>
Where even the evergreen cannot keep its leaves;<br>
None left to care about the passing minute:<br>
Yesterday I dreamt it. I wanted today<br>
To chase away the vision with the sight of you.<br><br>
1994
